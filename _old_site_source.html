Artist’s Impression

M Grand Minori celebrates the radiance of urban living, shaped by evolving lifestyles and inspired by the essence of Japanese philosophy.

Flexibility is key at these grand residences, which extend the possibilities of what a home can offer.

Golden Beam of Life

The Poetry of
Shoji & Light

Inspired by iki-mado concept – loosely translated as “window to live” – this design draws from the traditional shoji, the signature Japanese sliding door with latticed screens. From the delicate washi paper that covers the latticed panels to the graceful motion of sliding the doors open, each movement is an art form with its own purpose.

Passage to an

Illuminated Lifestyle

M Grand Minori is meticulously designed with pillars of Japanese-inspired elegance, where every space and experience is imbued with a signature Zen opulence.

Iki-Gen
Derived from the Japanese word genkan (玄関), meaning entryway, this design philosophy emphasises a grand arrival experience, setting the tone for prestige from the entrance to the lobby.
Iki-Gawa

Inspired by gawa (川), meaning water, this philosophy introduces a tranquil corridor experience, seamlessly blending serenity into a serviced apartment setting.

Iki-Niwa
Rooted in niwa (庭), meaning garden, this philosophy infuses the development’s facilities deck and common areas with the sophistication and ambience of exquisite Japanese garden design.
Iki- Mado
Drawing from mado (窓), meaning window, this philosophy prioritises expansive window designs, inviting an abundance of natural light into each home, fostering a luminous and airy living environment.
